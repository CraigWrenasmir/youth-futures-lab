Smart + Kind
Newcastle

You've been invited to spend a day redesigning your city.
Here's what to expect, and how to get ready.

Your mission

You are the
consultants now

On the day, your team works as a group of young consultants advising the council. Your job: choose one part of city life, and design an idea that would make Newcastle better for people your age.

This isn't a school project about "cities" in general. We want what you actually know about this one.

Talk about itIf the council genuinely listened to teenagers for one day — what should they hear?
The two words that matter

Every idea must be
smart and kind

Smart

A city that works well. Transport that turns up. Information you can find. Spaces that are actually designed, not left over.

Kind

A city that cares about people. Places you're welcome without spending money. Feeling safe without feeling watched. Room to be yourself.

Talk about itCan you think of something in Newcastle that's smart but not kind? Kind but not smart?
How the day works

The day has
three parts

Notice & choose

Morning — pick a focus area (transport, hangout spots, cost, safety, quiet spaces, creativity…), name a real problem, and start shaping an idea.

Design & build

Midday — turn your idea into something people can see: a poster, a map, an app mock-up, a campaign, a model. Materials provided.

Pitch

Afternoon — your team gives a 2-minute pitch to the room and a friendly listening panel. Coaching first. Nobody is thrown in cold.

How you'll share your idea

The pitch is
only three lines

Right now…what's the problem?
But what if…what's your idea?
Imagine if…what changes because of it?

It's two minutes long, the whole team can share the talking, and your poster does a lot of the work for you.

Your only homework

Start noticing
your city

No finished idea needed. No slides. No research assignment. Between now and the day, just pay attention as you move around Newcastle:

Where do young people feel welcome — or unwelcome?
How easy is it to actually get around?
What costs too much?
Where do you go to be with friends?
Where do you go when you need quiet?
Which places feel safe? Which feel uncomfortable?
What would make Newcastle better for teenagers?
What do adults miss or misunderstand?
Why you, specifically

You know things
adults don't

Adults plan this city. But you're the ones getting moved on from places, paying full fares, and looking for somewhere to just exist with your friends.

That's exactly what we want in the room. One of the questions your team will answer on the day:

"What do adults misunderstand about this issue?"
