TEDxNewy Youth Futures Lab · Teacher Overview

Smart + Kind Newcastle

A one-day civic design challenge where your students act as youth consultants advising a fictional council. The day finishes with short TED-style pitches.

DateFri 7 Aug 2026
Time9:30 – 2:30
Who~10 students per school
Teams5 – 6 students

1The short version

Students work in small consultancy teams. Each team chooses one part of city life that affects young people — transport, hangout spaces, creativity and more — designs an idea that would make Newcastle smarter and kinder, creates a simple visual to explain it, and delivers a 2-minute pitch to a friendly panel.
SmartA city that works well — systems, transport, technology, planning.
KindA city that cares about people — belonging, safety, access, affordability.

2What students will actually do

Morning

Notice & choose

Pick a focus area, name a real problem young people face, and generate ideas — including the question "what do adults misunderstand about this?"

Midday

Design & build

Turn the best idea into a proposal with a tangible asset — a poster, map, mock-up, campaign, or model — using materials we provide.

Afternoon

Pitch

Rehearse with coaching, then present a 2-minute TED-style pitch. All ideas will be collated into a web asset and shared with schools after the event.

Every pitch follows three lines: Right now… (the problem) → But what if… (the idea) → Imagine if… (what changes).

4Before the day

Students do not need to prepare an idea before they arrive. The only prep you might like to do is have a short conversation, getting them to start noticing their city:

  • Where do young people feel welcome — or unwelcome?
  • How easy is it to get around? What costs too much?
  • Where do you go to be with friends? Where do you go for quiet?
  • What places feel safe or uncomfortable?
  • What would make Newcastle better for teenagers?
  • What do adults often miss or misunderstand?
How students will benefit from the dayThis event is a communication workshop disguised as a hackathon. Through a collaborative design challenge, students will learn to develop, refine and clearly communicate a powerful idea. Maggie and Craig, both experienced TEDx communicators, will share practical strategies and personal insights that students can take into future presentations, projects and leadership opportunities.

5Logistics

  • All materials provided — including paper, pens, worksheets and focus-area cards. Students are welcome to bring a laptop or tablet for research and design activities. Familiar tools such as Canva may be useful, but no device or specific software is required.
  • Students from your school can work together — each team will be comprised of five students.
  • Teachers are encouraged to support and roam during workshops and be part of our listening panel.
  • Food: students should bring recess and lunch as they would on a normal school day. Brief outdoor breaks will be provided for eating. While takeaway options are available nearby, preparation and waiting times may make these unsuitable during the scheduled breaks.
